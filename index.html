Development of the Fetus

a basic walkthrough of how a single cell turns into a fully formed baby

Click on any word with a pink box around it to see a definition! Click on the word again to close the definition

Day 0:

  • Fertilization occurs within the fallopian tube
  • The nuceli of sperm and egg fuse

Day 1:

First cell divison occurs

Days 2-3:

Mitotic division to 4 cell stage, which is bound by zona pellucida

Day 4:

Cells have divided to 16

Day 5-7:

  • Zone hatching occurs
  • blastocyst development occurs

Month 1:

  • Skeletal system begins to form, as well as eyelids and ear buds
  • The placenta begins to form
Week 5: The yolk sac is created and viewable on an ultrasound

Weeks 6-7: The fetal pole is created and viewable on an ultrasound

Month 2:

  • Growth of limbs
  • Fingernails and tooth buds form
  • Genital development occurs
  • Kidneys become fully functional

Month 3:

  • Smaller bones begin to form
  • Nervous system begins to function
  • Reproductive organs are fully formed

Month 4

  • Fat begins to form
  • The fetus's skeleton starts changes from cartilage to bone

Month 5:

  • Fetus begins to start producing white blood cells
  • The fetus's skin begins to thicken

Month 6:

  • Increasing motor control and muscle development
  • Air sacs form in lungs

Month 7:

  • Nervous system maturation pace increases
  • Rapid brain growth occurs
  • Fetus starts sleeping

Month 8:

  • Fetus has fully developed bones
  • The fetus's body begins storing iron, calcium, and phosphorous
  • Organs other than lungs are fully functional

Month 9:

  • Weight gain in fetus slows
  • Fetus's lungs mature
  • Facial muscles of the fetus fully mature
